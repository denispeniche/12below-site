Private car sales go wrong in 4 ways

Every one of these costs buyers thousands. Escrow stops all of them.

⚠

Fake title

You hand over cash. The title turns out forged, salvage, or held by a lender. Car is repossessed weeks later.

⚠

Hidden lien

Seller still owes the bank. You pay them, they don’t pay off the loan, and the bank comes for the car — and you.

⚠

Wire fraud

Once funds wire, they’re gone. Seller ghosts. Bank can’t reverse it. Police can’t recover it.

⚠

Bait and switch

Photos showed an immaculate car. The actual car has frame damage, a salvage history, or a blown transmission.

Common questions

Most people start escrow within an hour of finding the car they want.

Do I need to use 12Below’s marketplace?
+
No. 12Below Escrow works for any private car sale. Found a car on Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace, AutoTrader, or just an ad in your neighborhood? We can hold the payment regardless of where the listing lives.
Who pays the $299 fee?
+
Either party. Most commonly the buyer pays, since the protection benefits them most. Some sellers offer to split or absorb the fee to signal trust to the buyer. You decide when you start the transaction.
How long does it take?
+
Setting up the escrow takes about 2 minutes. The whole transaction — from deposit, to inspection, to release — typically completes within 48 hours of the in-person meeting. Funds are released the same day you approve.
What if I inspect the car and don’t want it?
+
You cancel the transaction. Your full deposit is returned to your bank within 1–3 business days. The $299 fee is also fully refunded if the seller misrepresented the car. If you simply changed your mind, the $299 fee is non-refundable (this protects the seller’s time).
Is 12Below Escrow safe? Where does my money sit?
+
Funds are held in a segregated escrow account at our regulated payment processor (Dwolla). They are not commingled with 12Below operating funds. Dwolla is a US-registered money transmitter, bonded and licensed in every state where it operates.
What about the title? Who handles paperwork?
+
We provide state-specific bill of sale templates and title transfer walkthroughs for DC, Maryland, and Virginia. The seller signs the title in person at the meeting. You complete the title transfer at your local DMV after the escrow releases.
Does this work outside the DMV?
+
For now, 12Below Escrow only handles DMV-area transactions (DC, Maryland, Virginia). This lets us provide proper title-transfer support and reach you fast if anything goes wrong. We’ll expand once the DMV market is fully proven.
