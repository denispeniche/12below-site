Common questions about financing

Quick answers to the things buyers ask most often.

Does getting pre-qualified affect my credit score?
+
No. Pre-qualification uses a soft credit pull, which does not appear on your credit report and does not lower your score. You only get a hard inquiry later if you choose to formally apply for a specific loan amount.
Can I get financed with bad credit or no credit?
+
Our financing partner works with all credit profiles, including bad credit, no credit, and prior bankruptcies. Basic requirements: you are at least 18, earn at least $10,000/year, and have no active bankruptcy proceedings. Your rate will reflect your credit situation, but you have access.
What APR can I expect?
+
APR depends on your credit, the loan term, and the car. Buyers with strong credit (720+) often see rates from 4-6%. Average credit (660-719) tends to land 7-10%. Below 660, expect 11-18%. The pre-qualification flow gives you a real number tailored to you in under 2 minutes.
How much should I put as a down payment?
+
A common target is 10-20% down. On a 12Below car, 10% down often means a lower monthly payment than a comparable dealer purchase with 20% down — because you're financing a smaller starting amount. Use the payment calculator on any listing to compare scenarios in real time.
Can I use my pre-qualification at another dealer?
+
Yes — pre-qualification is yours. But remember: the financing only matters if the underlying car price is fair. A dealer with 5% APR on a car marked up $4,000 still costs more than 12Below at 7% APR on a car priced below market. Always compare the total cost, not just the rate.
How does the escrow protection work?
+
When you decide to buy, you connect your bank through Plaid (a bank-verification service trusted by millions). Your funds move into a secure escrow account — not directly to the seller. After you inspect the car in person and confirm everything matches the listing, you release the funds. If something is wrong, you cancel and the money goes back to you.
Is 12Below available outside the DMV?
+
Right now, every car on 12Below is located in DC, Maryland, or Virginia. This keeps inspection easy and removes the risks of buying a car sight-unseen across state lines. We plan to expand to additional metros once the DMV marketplace is established.
