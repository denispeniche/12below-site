The 12% rule

Buyers come for one reason: 12% below retail, every time.

Every 12Below listing is priced at 12% below current market retail value for that exact year, make, model, and mileage. Not 12% below MSRP. Not 12% below what some dealer down the road is asking. 12% below what the car actually sells for, today, in the DMV.

That single rule is the entire reason buyers show up. They search "used 2019 Honda Civic" on Google, see the same car at five DMV dealers for $14,500-$15,500, and find ours listed at $13,200. The decision is automatic.

You get retail-grade pricing without the retail-grade overhead. We get buyers who already trust the model. Nobody has to be convinced.
